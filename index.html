Siemens DISW, T&I - Advanced Solutions, R&D Developer

  • Use a code coverage measurement script to create a command to display code coverage of tests to evaluate effectiveness of said tests
  • Maintain components after release, fixing bugs when found by myself or other teammates and add requested features
Some technical and nontechnical skills used during the rotation include the following:

Summative Reflection

Throughout my academic and professional journey, I have grown tremendously, from a kid who wanted to code just because he enjoyed video games, to joining the robotics team in high school and becoming the code captain, and finally starting co-ops at one of the most prestigious tech companies in the world. I have learned many new skills, particularly from my robotics team, and especially from my co-op terms. Both of these experiences not only taught me new skills, such as basic robotics coding, Android development, and web development, and allowed me to work with technology I would never be able to work with otherwise, but improved my teamwork and leadership teams, and taught me how to be a developer that others want to work with. I am very excited to continue to learn new skills and expand my network, and continue to strive to be a developer and a person people enjoy working with, and hopefully will be able to use my business skills in a position in the future.

Through the co-op experience, I have learned new skills much faster than I would otherwise be able to. It has been a very positive experience and given me a chance to use the skills I have learned in school in a professional environment. During my co-ops with Siemens, I have done both Android development with the Google Glass EE2 and web development for components in our web library, two areas that I was unfamiliar with before starting to co-op. These have given me an invaluable opportunity to learn from professional developers like my mentor, and I have learned much quicker than if I was at school. Overall, my co-op experience has been very educational for both my technical and nontechnical skill development, and allowed me to work with accomplished developers and learn from the best.

The co-op program has been both fulfilling and affirming for me in my professional career. Before co-oping, I had never developed in a professional environment, so being granted the opportunity to do this was very helpful in determining that development was the career I wanted to pursue. The co-op program was also fulfilling, as I felt that my work actually mattered for the team, and I wasn't just given filler tasks. I worked on real projects with my mentors, and was given feedback from both my team and other teams for some of them. I am very proud of the work I did during my terms, and I hope to continue this high-quality work in the future.

If I was to start over again, the only thing I would change is to be in-person for my co-ops instead of online. I learn much better in a physical environment, so if I wasn't restricted by COVID, I would want to do my terms in-person.

For my remaining co-op terms, I want to focus on learning new skills each semester. One of my short-term goals is to be a full-stack developer, which will require me to have a wide skillset. I do not want to do the same thing in any two co-op terms, to continue to expand my knowledge base. I also want to expand my network, and try to branch out to create many connections as possible. Networking is an incredibly important part of professional development, especially business, and if I want to become a CTO one day, I need to create these connections.
